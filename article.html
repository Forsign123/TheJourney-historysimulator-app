How We Interpolated Captain Cook's GPS Coordinates

Published by Codesign Projects • 5 min read

When we set out to build The Journey, the goal was simple: create a real-time simulator of the 1768 HMS Endeavour expedition. We had the verbatim journal entries, we had the historical dates, but we quickly ran into a massive technical wall regarding the map.

Captain James Cook was a master navigator, but in the 18th century, a ship's log didn't record minute-by-minute GPS coordinates. Sometimes, days would pass in the open ocean with only a single coordinate noted at noon.

The Dead Reckoning Problem

If we just plotted the exact coordinates recorded in the Admiralty logs, our digital ship would "teleport" across the map once a day. That ruins the immersion of a real-time simulator. We needed the ship to move smoothly across the UI, 24 hours a day.

"Moderate and fair weather... hoisted the Pendant, and took charge of the Ship... From this day to the 21st of July we were constantly employed in fitting the Ship."

— Excerpt from the July 1768 Log

To solve this, we had to rely on the same technique the sailors used: Dead Reckoning.

Building the Interpolation Engine

We created an algorithm that reads the distance between Point A (Monday's noon coordinate) and Point B (Tuesday's noon coordinate). But a straight line isn't historically accurate—ships tack against the wind.

Notice the subtle curves in the tracking path—this represents our wind-adjusted interpolation.

By feeding our engine the historical wind direction and speed notes from Cook’s log margins, the app mathematically generates a curved, realistic sailing path. Now, when you open the app at 3:00 AM, the GPS tracker knows exactly where the ship is located in the middle of the Atlantic, down to the exact degree and minute.

Bridging History and Tech

It was a painstaking process of cross-referencing modern mapping APIs with 250-year-old handwriting. But the result is a seamless, modern UI that honors the incredible navigational achievements of the original crew.
