Relive The Age of Discovery

Commissioned by the Royal Society in 1768, Captain James Cook set sail on the HMS Endeavour to observe the Transit of Venus and search for the elusive Terra Australis Incognita. The Journey is a first-of-its-kind historical simulator that allows you to experience this legendary maritime expedition in true real-time.

Verbatim Historical Journals

Read the exact 18th-century observations directly from Captain Cook’s personal logs. The app syncs with the modern calendar, delivering day-for-day entries from the fitting at Deptford Yard to navigating the treacherous Great Barrier Reef.

Interpolated GPS Tracking

Using advanced interpolation of historical recorded speeds, wind direction, and dead reckoning, our mapping engine charts the exact wake of the ship across global waters, bridging the gap between history and modern telemetry.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the real-time simulator work?

The app aligns the current calendar date with the 1768-1771 timeline. If Captain Cook recorded a storm off the coast of Brazil on today's date in 1768, you will unlock and read that exact journal entry today.

Do I need an internet connection to use the map?

No. The app includes global offline maps (Zoom levels 0-5), allowing you to track the HMS Endeavour's coordinates and interpolated path anywhere in the world, completely offline.

Are the journal entries historically accurate?

Yes. Every log entry, coordinate, and remark is transcribed verbatim from the original Admiralty logs and Captain James Cook's personal journals kept during the first voyage of discovery.
